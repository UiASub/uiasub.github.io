TAC Challenge

Where concept meets reality

TAC Challenge is an international ROV/AUV competition for students from around the world. The competition focuses on developing innovative solutions for subsea operations and gives participants the opportunity to test their skills in a realistic environment.

Developed by Stinger Technology, the competition consists of several missions that test both autonomous and piloted skills in underwater technology.

1. Subsea Docking

A docking station is positioned in an indoor training pool. The vehicle must locate the station and attempt to dock. The station is equipped with an inductive 250W Subsea Power Puck that can transfer data and power.

Deliverables:

  • Successful demonstration of docking to the docking station
  • Precision docking (Power Puck connection)
  • Successful demonstration of power transfer

Technical details:

  • Docking station: EUR-pallet size (1200Ã—800mm)
  • ArUco markers in corners (ID: 28, 7, 19, 96)
  • Subsea USB Power Puck (BB8290 primary, BB8813 secondary)
  • Magnetic steel plates for assisted docking
+20p Standard Docking +50p Precision Docking +50p Power Transfer +100p Autonomous Bonus

2. Pipeline Inspection

A pipeline positioned on the seabed in the operational area needs to be inspected. The pipeline has an unknown path and position. There is an unknown number of ArUco markers along the pipeline that must be identified.

Deliverables:

  • A list of marker IDs in the correct order

Technical details:

  • Pipeline: YELLOW color, 200mm diameter, max 10m length
  • 4-10 ArUco markers (ID 1-99)
  • Acoustic pinger (30kHz) at pipeline start
  • Markers minimum 0.2m apart
+10p per correct marker +25p Correct order +25p Correct starting point +10p per marker (autonomous) +100p Autonomous Tracking

3 & 4. Visual Inspection + Valve Intervention

These two missions are performed in the same run.

Visual Inspection:

An underwater structure on the seabed must be inspected. Various ArUco markers are positioned on the structure and must be identified.

  • Structure: RAL 1004 (golden yellow), RGB = 228, 158, 0
  • 5-10 ArUco markers (ID 1-99, may occur multiple times)
  • Points: +20p per correct marker, -10p per incorrect marker
  • Bonus: +20p per marker (autonomous detection)

Valve Intervention:

Two valves are positioned on the same underwater structure. The valves have a standardized subsea interface and can be rotated 90 degrees.

  • Valves: RAL 2004 (pure orange), RGB = 226, 83, 3
  • Standardized subsea valve (120mm outer radius)
  • Valve A: vertical surface, Valve B: horizontal surface
  • Points: +50p per valve
  • Bonus: +100p per valve (autonomous operation)

Autonomous Bonus Points

Extra bonus points are awarded if specific autonomous behavior is demonstrated. If the team intends to attempt these specific executions, they must notify the judges during the mission briefing.

Although autonomous behavior awards extra points, piloted solutions will also be rewarded. Teams may freely choose which missions to attempt, but it is strongly encouraged to attempt all missions to some extent.
