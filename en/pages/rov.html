Technical Specifications

K2 represents next-generation ROV technology with a modular design focused on field operations and competitions.

8 Vectorized Thrusters

Precise vector control provides fine maneuvering in all axes for inspection and station-keeping.

6 Degrees of Freedom (6 DOF)

Full freedom of movement for complex tasks, including pitch, yaw and roll.

Modular Power System

Quick battery pack replacement and modular connectors for field maintenance.

Below you'll find detailed specifications divided into hardware, sensor suite, control & capabilities, and software integration.

Hardware Specifications

The K2 frame is built from corrosion-resistant aluminum alloy with a vectorized thruster layout for maximum maneuverability. Electronics are housed in watertight enclosures with quick service connectors.

The thruster configuration uses commercially available, field-tested motors with vectorized brackets.

  • Frame: 6061-T6 aluminum, anodized
  • Dimensions (L√óW√óH): 800mm √ó 600mm √ó 400mm
  • Weight in air: ~25kg; weight in water: ~2kg (slightly positive buoyancy)
  • Thrusters: 8 √ó Blue Robotics T200, max thrust 5.25 kgf
  • Electronics: Custom PCB, NUCLEO-F767ZI (STM32F767)
  • Depth rating: 100m operational, 150m max

Software and Integration

We follow an open-source philosophy: firmware, topside apps and tools are available in our GitHub repositories for collaboration and further development.

  • Firmware: Zephyr RTOS on NUCLEO-F767ZI (C/C++), Zephyr v4.2
  • Network: Static IP 192.168.1.100, Subnet 255.255.255.0, UDP port 12345
  • Threads (example): Main, UDP, Control (PID), CANbus
  • UDP packet: uint32_t sequence, uint64_t payload, uint32_t crc32 (CRC32 validation)
  • Payload (64-bit) bitplan (high-level): Surge, Sway, Heave, Roll, Pitch, Yaw, Light, Manipulator (8 bits each)
  • Protocol: Custom binary over UDP with CRC32 check
  • Topside: Electron app, cross-platform
  • Video: H.264, low latency (<100ms)
  • Logging: CSV/binary with synchronized timestamps
  • Repos: K2-Zephyr, Topside

Project Timeline

From idea to reality - our journey with underwater technology

2023 - Establishment

UiASub is Born

The student group UiASub was founded at the University of Agder with the goal of building autonomous underwater vehicles.

  • First team members recruited
  • Established collaboration with UiA
  • Started planning the first ROV
  • Applied for sponsor support
Completed

2024 - First Competition

TAC Challenge Participation

Participated in the TAC Challenge, an international ROV competition for students. Valuable experience with practical testing and the competitive environment.

  • Built and tested the first ROV prototype
  • Competed in the TAC Challenge
  • Learned from experienced teams around the world
  • Identified improvement areas for K2
Completed

2025 - K2 Development

Next Generation ROV

Started development of the K2 ROV with 8 vectorized thrusters, 6 DOF, and modular design. Focus on field operations and competition performance.

  • Designed a new aluminum frame structure
  • Implemented Zephyr RTOS firmware
  • Developed an Electron-based topside app
  • Integrated an advanced sensor package
Ongoing

2026 - Completion

K2 ROV Ready for Action

Expected completion of the K2 ROV with full testing and validation. Ready for competitions and field missions.

  • Complete system integration and testing
  • Deep-water testing up to 100m
  • Participation in international competitions
  • Documentation and open-source publication
